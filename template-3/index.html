About the hub

A bridge between research and everyday practice

This hub brings together educators, coordinators, psychologists, and families who want gifted education to be grounded in evidence and compassion. We focus on the real decisions people make every day: in classrooms, at kitchen tables, and in leadership meetings.

Use this template to share your own story, your team, and the commitments that shape the way you work with advanced learners.

Focus areas

Where we put our energy

Classroom design

Practical ways to make everyday lessons more open, flexible, and challenging for high-ability learners.

  • Flexible grouping and pacing
  • Tiered tasks and menus
  • Opportunities for depth

Social and emotional care

Supporting identity, wellbeing, and friendships for gifted learners who may feel different to their peers.

  • Belonging and peer connection
  • Perfectionism and anxiety
  • Strength-based conversations

Family partnership

Creating simple, respectful ways for families and schools to share insights and plan together.

  • Shared language and goals
  • Student-led meetings
  • Home + school continuity

Systems and pathways

Looking at the bigger picture: identification, transitions, and opportunities across a school or region.

  • Inclusive identification approaches
  • Program design and review
  • Policy with lived experience
